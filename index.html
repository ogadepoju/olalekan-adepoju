About Olalekan Adepoju

I am an educator and researcher specializing in rhetoric, composition, and international writing pedagogy. With a PhD from the University of Louisville, my work focuses on empowering multilingual writers and decolonizing academic writing spaces through transnational approaches.

Currently serving as an Instructor at the College of Staten Island, CUNY, I design and teach writing courses that recognize linguistic diversity as an asset. My research explores how writing instruction can better serve international and multilingual students by valuing their existing skills.

About Me

I am a passionate educator and researcher dedicated to advancing writing pedagogy through transnational and multilingual perspectives. My work focuses on recognizing and mobilizing the linguistic and cultural assets of international writers, particularly in writing center contexts.

With a PhD in Rhetoric and Composition from the University of Louisville and extensive experience teaching across diverse student populations, I bring both theoretical knowledge and practical classroom expertise to my work. My research examines how writing instruction can better serve international and multilingual students by valuing their existing skills rather than focusing on perceived deficits.

🎯 Teaching Philosophy

  • Inclusive classroom practices
